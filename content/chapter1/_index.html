This section will be referring to a classic operations research textbook (Winston 2004), (Margalit and Rabinoff 2019), and a linear algebra book with R and Python code supplements (Yoshida 2021). Linear algebra is essential to many optimization techniques in addition to statistics in general (especially predictive modeling). In many (if not all industries), data will be formatted in vectors and/or matrices. For those who have worked with spreadsheets before, you already have seen them in those forms as tables. Typically, the numbers in those tables will involve total constraints, metrics of a variable (coefficients), etc. This is designed to be a basic introduction to linear algebra towards developing working knowledge. These topics include:

  • Vectors Spaces
  • Systems of Linear Equations
  • Linear Independence and Dependence
  • Determinants
  • Linear Transformations
  • Diagonalization, Eigenvalues, and Eigenvectors

References

Margalit, Dan, and Joseph Rabinoff. 2019. Interactive Linear Algebra. Georgia Tech.
Winston, Wayne L. 2004. Operations Research: Applications and Algorithms. 4th ed. Duxbury Press.
Yoshida, Ruriko. 2021. Linear Algebra and Its Applications with R. Taylor & Francis Group.
