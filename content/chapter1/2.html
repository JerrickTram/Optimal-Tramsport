Designing a System of Linear Equations

How exactly is linear algebra a key component in many optimization and statistics problems? The answer is solving the title of this section. In most optimization problems, we want to find the best way to allocate resources to accomplish a goal. Not the largest/smallest nor longest/shortest way, but optimal. Deriving from an example in (Yoshida 2021), an industrial gas company wants to transport gas canisters from their production sites (A, B) to their distributors (C, D). Distributor C have a demand of 500 canisters/week and 400 canisters/week for Distributor D. In addition, production sites A and B can supply 600 and 500 canisters/week respectively. Formulating the system of linear equations:

\[\begin{align} x_{AC} + x_{AD} = 600 \\ x_{BC} + x_{BD} = 500 \\ x_{AC} + x_{BC} = 500 \\ x_{AD} + x_{BD} = 400 \end{align}\]
