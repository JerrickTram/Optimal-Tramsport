If you’ve ever heard of redundancy, you’re probably heard about it in two different lights:

  • In a just in case (JIC) situation, that inventory management strategy focuses on keeping some products at hand to avoid shortages. With the abundance of shortages nowadays with COVID playing a role in port backlogs, this strategy might be preferable at this time.
  • In a just in time (JIT) situation, that inventory management strategy prioritizes removing unnecessary waste and increasing efficiency.

In the case of linear independence, we’re referring to the 2nd option. We want to understand all vectors from a set in all real numbers such that the vector equation has a possible solution. In most cases, that solution would normally be the key to solving a system of linear equations.

\[\begin{align} \text{Span}\{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_i \} = \{a_1x_1 + a_2x_2 + \dots + a_ix_i | x_1, x_2, \dots, x_i \in \mathbb{R}\} \end{align}\]
